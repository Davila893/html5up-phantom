Marian Rejewski was a Polish mathematician and cryptologist who reconstructed the Nazi German military Enigma cipher machine sight-unseen in the 1932. The intelligence that was gained by the British from Enigma decrypts formed part of what was code named Ultra and contributed-perhaps decisively-to the defeat of Germany. In September 1932, Rejewski attend a secret cryptology course. Marian Rejewski deduced the machine’s secret internal wiring after only a few weeks. He and his two colleagues then developed successive techniques for the regular decryption of Enigma message. He went silence after the war was over, but he showed his work, at the age of 74 he died of a heart attack. The Enigma machine was an electromechanical device, equipped with a 26-letter keyboard and 26 lamps, corresponding to the letters of the alphabet. ENIAC

ENIAC stands electronic numerical integrator and computers. It was the oldest technology ever made in 1964. In 1964 it had the nickname of giant brain by the press. It contained 20,000 vacuum tubes, 7,200, 1,500 relays, 70,000 resistors, 10,000 capacitors and 5,000,000 hand-soldered joints.
